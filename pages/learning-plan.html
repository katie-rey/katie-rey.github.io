My Learning Plan


What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?


My long term goal and/or career pathway is to work full time in computer programming. I developed this pathway by getting to understand more about myself, what made me tick, what I enjoyed. Spending many years in a customer facing role, I came to realise I much preferred being in front of a computer. I love problem solving, researching and coding. I get a bit lost in a good way in it.


I would like to become a junior developer in a larger team. Working in a supportive environment is super important to me, having worked in some not so supportive environments.


Description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning


From my research, I have discovered my strengths are my problem solving skills, empathy for others, passion, and a tenacity to keep on learning.


Having an understanding, my key strength is problem solving has definitely helped confirm for me computer programming would be a great career path. My tenacity to keep on learning has always been a strength, always wanting to push myself into something new. As my partner says “by hook or by crook, I’ll get the job done".


My passion is something that really drives me. When I’m passionate about something, I get super excited and engaged.


My limitations are lack of self-confidence, public speaking, procrastination and at times attention to detail.


My procrastination has definitely had an effect on my learning, leaving tasks to the last minute. This is something I am aware of and am working towards changing. I am developing better time management strategies and tying to make myself accountable/responsible for my actions.


Self confidence and public speaking go hand in hand. I’m really good one on one but tend to pull back within a group setting. I am actively trying to change this and want to engage more with the Slack channel.


What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?


I would like to develop my group participation and oration built around self confidence. I would also like to develop my active listening skills by being more patient.


A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include: • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives


My commitment on managing my workload: I will be on time and present everyday. I will be open minded to new ideas and patient. I will be kind and supportive, also allow myself to be supported where it is needed.


What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you


When things are building up and getting to me, I will take time in that moment to clear my head and grab a cup of tea. I’m committed to seeking help and communicating with others around me.


A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way


I am committed to seeking help once I have exhausted all personal avenues (as I really enjoy researching and problem solving). I will try to spend 1-2 hours on a problem, then seek help on Slack first. If I still require help, I will communicate with my facilitator.


A description of what you expect from the facilitation team


I expect support in both tech and core, active listening and honest constructive feedback and criticism.

I do not have any current times to block-out, however my dog will miss me if I don’t walk him.
