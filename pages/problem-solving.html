Problem Solving

Discuss when you were blocked on a simple problem

Golly gosh - last week I felt blocked on many occasions. As I told my facilitator, I felt like I had been thrown into a deep pool and couldn't swim....and sharks were circling.

My biggest block this week was based around the GradeBook challenge - trying to add an array inside an object and then accessing that information. To push a new score into the testScores array, I firstly over-complicated the function by adding in a loop. To solve the problem, I Googled the solution, compared three or more sites that explained the push() method and tried to establish its syntax. I then set up a separate js & html page in VSCode to practice and troubleshoot the errors in the console.

I also did a bit more research on dot and bracket notation..."bracket notation is another way to access a property of an object".

Throughout the process I kept on feeling frustrated. I chose to takes brakes and sleep on a problem if it was taking too long. This week however was very different to last week. Instead of getting overwhelmed and letting a fixed mindset creep in, I choose to stay positive, relaxed and nurture my growth mindset mentality.

A simple problem I solve in an elegant way was achieved completing the deBuzz problem. Instead of adding in each variant of the word buzz to filter (buzz, BUzz, buZZ - which I did initially), I converted the word toLowerCase.

Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem solving techniques/processes:

Pseudocode

I wasn't that confident writing pseudocode code. What I have realised about my learning process is that I tend to rush in with test code before spending time to work out the problem step by step. This week I spent more time reflecting on the problem before jumping in.

Trying something

I feel very confident trying something. I tend to jump in head first and see what happens. Ths can be a positive and a negative process.

Rubber ducky method

I am only starting to use this technique. I have been using my partner as a bouncing board to talk to and found it help a lot.

Reading error messages

I feel comfortable reading errors messages and this has formed a large part of my problem solving arsenal this week.

Console.logging

I had a revelation this week and realised how useful and important console.logging is! I will be using it a lot more in the future to test my code and see if I'm on the right track.

Googling

I Google a lot and tend to find myself down a few rabbit holes with way to many tabs open. This is my primary problem solving technique which I find most useful.

Asking your peers for help/Asking coaches for help

I am not the greatest at asking for help from my peers and coach. This is something I am actively working on and will try to improve on during Sprint 5.

Improving your process with reflection

I have found reflecting with my partner very valuable. I have spent time discussing my work process and have discovered new ideas and strategies to implement. This was super helpful in understanding myself further.
